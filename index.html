Six conventional NDT methods, delivered to international standards.

We support projects in marine, oil & gas, petrochemical, construction, power and process sectors. Each method is selected by the asset, the defect mechanism and the access constraints — and delivered by qualified inspectors under our ISO/IEC 17025:2017 scope.

UT

Ultrasonic Testing (UT)

High-frequency sound waves for internal flaw detection & thickness measurement — welds, corrosion monitoring, structural assessments.

ASME V · ART 5
RT

Radiographic Testing (RT)

X-ray or gamma rays for inspecting pressure vessels, pipeline welds, castings & structural fabrication — permanent inspection records.

ASME V · ART 2
MPI

Magnetic Particle Inspection

Surface & near-surface discontinuities in ferromagnetic materials — crack detection, weld inspection, fabrication QA.

ASME V · ART 7
PT

Penetrant Testing (PT)

Surface-breaking defects in non-porous materials — welds, castings, machined components, aerospace & precision parts.

ASME V · ART 6
PMI

Positive Material ID (PMI)

Alloy composition & material traceability via XRF or OES — piping systems, refineries, petrochemical, asset verification.

XRF · OES
HT

Hardness Testing

Resistance to indentation & wear — Brinell, Rockwell, Vickers. Heat-treatment validation, weld QC, failure analysis.

BRINELL · ROCKWELL · VICKERS

Phased Array UT (PAUT)

Electronic beam steering for detailed imaging — weld inspection, corrosion analysis, complex geometries. High accuracy & faster inspection.

ASME V · ART 4

HIGH POD · DIGITAL

Common questions.

Which industries does VES support with NDT services?

We support marine, oil & gas, petrochemical, construction, power and process sectors. Our qualified inspectors are deployed for onshore and offshore projects, asset-integrity programmes and project-based fabrication QA.

Are your NDT services accredited?

Yes. VES operates under ISO/IEC 17025:2017 for testing & calibration, and under ISO/IEC 17020:2012 for inspection bodies. All testing is carried out in full compliance with international codes and standards.

What advanced NDT techniques do you provide?

Phased Array Ultrasonic Testing (PAUT), Time-of-Flight Diffraction (TOFD) and automated corrosion mapping. These methods improve defect detection accuracy, reduce downtime and provide permanent digital records.

Can you mobilise across regions for unplanned campaigns?

Yes. VES operates through its Singapore headquarters with branch offices in Saudi Arabia (VES Arabia LLC) and the Philippines (VES Philippines), supported by a global network of trusted associates — enabling fast response across regions and regulatory environments.

How do I request a quotation?

Send your scope of work, drawing pack and target schedule. A qualified VES engineer will respond with a method recommendation, mobilisation plan and price within 1–3 business days.

Certification pathway

From Level I to Level III —
a structured ladder.

All ISO 9712 NDT programmes follow a three-tier progression. Each level builds on the practical and theoretical foundation of the previous one, supported by hands-on exposure on industrial inspection equipment and sample components.

L I

Level I — Foundation

Introduces participants to fundamentals of NDT methods and inspection procedures. Classroom instruction combined with practical demonstrations to develop basic inspection competency.

  • Introduction to NDT & safety procedures
  • Equipment familiarisation & calibration basics
  • Inspection techniques & defect identification
  • Codes, standards & reporting procedures

Qualification: Performs inspections under supervision per certification requirements.

L II

Level II — Independent

Advanced inspection knowledge and independent testing capability. Builds technique-selection judgement and interpretation skill across NDT methods.

  • Advanced inspection procedures & technique selection
  • Defect interpretation & equipment calibration
  • Code interpretation & inspection reporting
  • Supervision of Level I personnel

Qualification: Conducts inspections independently, interprets results, prepares technical reports.

L III

Level III — Leadership

Senior inspection professionals pursuing advanced certification and technical leadership. Procedure ownership and quality-system accountability.

  • Procedure development & standards interpretation
  • Quality-system requirements

Training FAQ

Common training questions.

Who can attend the training programmes?

Training programmes are open to technicians, engineers, inspectors, students and industry professionals — from entry-level participants pursuing first certification to experienced practitioners renewing or upgrading credentials.

Are courses available online?

Yes — selected programmes are available through remote and hybrid learning methods. Live instructor sessions, recorded materials, virtual assessments and interactive Q&A are supported. Practical sessions for applicable technical programmes are run in-centre or onsite.

Are certifications internationally recognised?

Yes. Programmes align with internationally recognised standards and certification schemes — ISO 9712 for NDT, CSWIP / AWS for welding inspection, API for code-driven inspection, and recognised safety regulators for HSE.

Is practical training included?

Yes — hands-on practical sessions are included for applicable technical programmes (UT, RT, MPI, PT, VT, advanced NDT, welding inspection, coating inspection and safety). Participants train on industrial inspection equipment and sample components.

How can I register?
